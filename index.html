How it works

  1. 1

    A person verifies once

    Full KYC — documents and selfie — happens a single time, performed by a specialist verification provider. Kvick ID never stores the documents; they stay with the provider that checked them.

  2. 2

    A platform asks

    When the same person signs up somewhere new, that platform asks Kvick ID one question: is this person verified? It learns nothing — not even whether an account exists — until the person answers.

  3. 3

    The person decides

    Share once, share for a period, or decline — every reuse requires explicit consent, every consent is recorded, and consent can be withdrawn at any time.

  4. 4

    The platform gets a signed answer

    A cryptographically signed status — verified, at what level, by which provider, valid until when. Evidence the platform can file and stand behind, for its own compliance decision. Kvick ID supplies the evidence; the decision stays with the platform.

That is the complete payload. Signed, timestamped, and tied to the specific consent that authorised it — so what was shared, and when, is provable later by either side.

Never included

  • ×No identity documents. Kvick ID holds none, so it can pass none.
  • ×No personal details beyond the verification status itself.
  • ×No mention of where the person verified first. Platforms learn nothing about each other's customers through us.
  • ×No answer at all without consent — asking about any email address looks identical from the outside until the person says yes.

Where this stands

Kvick ID is in development, and we would rather say so plainly than imply otherwise. The system works end to end today — verification, consent, reuse, revocation, and a tamper-evident audit trail — running entirely on synthetic test data while formal legal review is prepared.

European by design
All personal data is hosted in EU data centres. The operating company is being established in the EU.
Built with the rules in mind
Designed around EU AML and GDPR requirements from the first line of code. We make no compliance claims before legal review is complete — by policy.
Neutral infrastructure
No platform gets preferential treatment, and none learns about another's customers through us. Neutrality is the product.
